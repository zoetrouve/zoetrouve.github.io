About Me

Hi, My name is Zoé and I am a senior at Georgetown University living and working in my townhome in Georgetown. I have had much more time to cook this past year given the pandemic. Thus, I have collected my favorite recipes and organized them into a simple webpage to be accessed quickly and easily.


Breakfast

My go-to breakfast is an oatmeal I have perfected over the course of this year. I have eaten various versions of this oatmeal almost every day. I start with boiling 1 cup of water on the stove. As the water is boiling, I dice a small apple into half-inch cubes. When the water reaches boiling, I add the apple to the pot along with a half-cup of dry oats. Then, I add a couple shakes of cinnamon and a handful of frozen blueberries. I let it sit and stir occasionally to make sure all the flavors meld together beautifully.

Dinner

Dinner for me growing up was the classic French family meal. Two hours long and just sitting and chatting with my siblings and parents. My mom is a wonderful chef and always cooked incredible dinners. This past year I have been more intentional than ever about learning some tips and tricks from the queen herself. I found out about this specific recipe from one of my roomates who has cooked it a lot. I made it for my Mom over winterbreak and it was #momapproved. This Shakshuka can be a classic breakfast (or in my case, dinner) dish that pairs well with eggs and feta as optional add-ins. This easy, quick recipe will feed your stomach for days to come. You can even change up how it's prepared as left-overs such as in an omelet or on top of a bed of lettuce. You will not be disappointed.

Dessert

Baking has always been a favorite past-time of mine. I do not get to bake as often as I would like when I am at school so whenever I am home I never pass up the opportunity. This time of year, I absolutely love making homemade Easter cookies. They are just simple sugar cookie base and royal icing frosting but they are so delicious and so easy to make. I have worked on my decorating skills, especially since the start of college, and believe that this past year was a high point for my Easter cookies. I cannot wait to see what this coming year has to bring to the table (in terms of my decorating skills of course).
